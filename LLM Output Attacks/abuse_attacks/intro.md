### Introduction to Abuse Attacks 

Abuse attacks involve malicious actors deliberately using Large Language Models (LLMs) to generate and spread harmful or misleading content at scale. Unlike hallucinations, which are accidental inaccuracies, abuse attacks intentionally aim to deceive, manipulate, or harm individuals, organizations, or societies.

#### Propaganda and Psychological Manipulation
LLMs can be used to mass-produce propaganda, biased narratives, fake testimonials, and persuasive content designed to influence public opinion or promote ideological agendas. Attackers may also deploy AI-powered social media bots that mimic real users and engage in conversations, amplifying misinformation and enabling large-scale influence campaigns, including potential election interference.

#### Cybersecurity Threats and Fraud
LLMs can enhance phishing, impersonation, and social engineering attacks by generating convincing emails, messages, or notices that appear legitimate. This can trick individuals or employees into revealing sensitive data or transferring funds. Additionally, LLMs may be used to automate harassment or coordinated abuse campaigns.

#### Misinformation, Fake Reviews, and Defamation
Malicious users can generate fake reviews, defamatory articles, or fabricated scandals targeting individuals, businesses, or institutions. LLMs can also create convincing fake news, conspiracy theories, or misleading narratives that undermine trust in institutions, manipulate public opinion, or damage reputations. Because this content can be generated rapidly at scale, it can spread faster than fact-checkers can respond.

#### Hate Speech Generation
LLMs may unintentionally generate biased or hateful content due to patterns in training data. Adversaries can exploit this by crafting prompts that bypass safety filters to mass-produce offensive or extremist rhetoric. This capability enables the rapid spread of hate speech across online platforms, potentially fueling division and radicalization.
